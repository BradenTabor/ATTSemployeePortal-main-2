# Geo-Boy Certification — Study Guide

## Overview

This guide covers key topics for the Geo-Boy brush mulching certification test. Use it alongside hands-on training.

## Safety Distances

Learn these critical distances:

- **10 feet** — Minimum from hazards, poles, and guy wires
- **6 feet** — Minimum from drop offs or unstable ground
- **2 spans** — Stay away from downed power lines
- **300 feet** — Buffer zone for safety flap use and from personnel
- **25 degrees** — Maximum sideling operation angle

## Safety Features

Know these Geo-Boy safety features and their purposes:

1. **Windshield** — Protects from debris
2. **Backup beeper** — Alerts people when backing
3. **Backup camera** — Allows safe reversing
4. **Grab handles** — Assists ascending/descending
5. **Flashing lights** — Visibility in low light conditions

## Required PPE

- Ear plugs (minimum)
- Safety glasses
- Hard hat
- Work gloves

## Daily Operations

### Startup
- Complete equipment inspection daily before operation
- Perform function test before mulching
- Allow proper warm-up time

### Mulching Safety
- Keep safety flap closed unless 300ft from people/property
- Never mulch in ditches or drainages
- Mulch facing away from roadways (backward motion)
- Stop immediately if anyone enters 300ft buffer zone

### Shutdown
- Wait for mowing head to completely stop before exiting
- Never exit while head is still rotating
- Remove debris and fire hazards from machine

## Maintenance

- **Grease moving parts** — Every 8 hours
- **Check mowing head teeth** — Twice daily

## Common Accidents & Prevention

| Accident | Prevention |
|----------|------------|
| Flying debris | Keep safety flap closed when needed |
| Roll overs | Stay within 25 degrees on level gauges |
| Falls | Maintain 3 points of contact ascending/descending |

## Key Rules

- **Everyone** has stop work authority
- No passengers allowed on/in the Geo-Boy
- Never leave mowing head rotating while roading
- Both operator and groundman responsible for hazard identification
- Maintain 3 points of contact when mounting/dismounting

## Practice

Take the certification test from **Resources → Certifications** when you're ready.
