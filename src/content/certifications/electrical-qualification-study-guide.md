# Electrical Qualification (1910.269) — Study Guide

## Overview

This guide covers key topics for the Electrical Qualification test based on **OSHA 29 CFR 1910.269(r)** (line-clearance tree trimming) and related requirements. Use it alongside hands-on training and your company’s safety procedures. The test assesses knowledge needed to work safely near electric power generation, transmission, and distribution lines.

**Regulatory references:** OSHA 29 CFR 1910.269, 1910.333(c)(3); ANSI Z133 (arboricultural safety).

---

## Three Qualification Levels

OSHA recognizes three qualification levels for work near electric power lines:

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Unqualified** | Electrically unqualified. Must maintain at least **10 feet** from overhead power lines (50 kV and below). 1910.269(r) line-clearance provisions do not apply; Subpart S (e.g. 1910.333(c)(3)) applies. |
| **Line-clearance tree trimmer** | Received specialized training to work within 10 feet of energized lines. Must follow 1910.269(r)(1) and related paragraphs (training, job briefing, PPE, MAD tables, insulating tools, adverse weather, two-person rule). |
| **269-qualified** | Knowledgeable in construction/operation of electric power equipment and hazards. Nearly all of 1910.269 applies except (r)(1); they use 269-specific skills and MAD procedures. |

---

## Minimum Approach Distances (MAD)

**Source:** OSHA 29 CFR 1910.269, **Tables R-5, R-6, R-7, and R-8**. Always confirm current values in the actual regulation.

- **Unqualified workers:** MAD starts at **10 feet** for systems **50 kV and below**; increases **4 inches** for every **10 kV over 50 kV** (1910.333(c)(3)(i)).
- **Line-clearance tree trimmers:** Body, tools, ladders, platforms, and aerial devices must stay **outside the MAD** from energized parts per Tables R-5 through R-8 (1910.269(r)(1)(iii), (r)(1)(v)).
- **Voltage:** Before work, determine the **nominal voltage** of lines posing a hazard—or assume **all lines at the maximum voltage** present (1910.269(r)(1)(i)).

## Line-Clearance Tree Trimming — Key Rules

### Before Work

- Determine **voltage** of lines that may pose a hazard, or treat all as **maximum voltage**.
- **Job briefing** before starting and if conditions change (1910.269(c)).
- **Medical and first aid** provisions of 1910.269(b) apply.

### During Work

- **MAD:** Maintain distances from energized conductors per **Tables R-5, R-6, R-7, R-8** for body, ladders, platforms, aerial devices (1910.269(r)(1)(iii), (v)).
- **Insulating equipment:** Branches **in contact with** or **within MAD** of energized parts may be removed **only with insulating equipment** (1910.269(r)(1)(iv)). Tools must be **clean and dry** to count as insulated where the employer demonstrates insulating qualities per (j)(1).

### Two-Person Rule (1910.269(r)(1)(ii))

A **second line-clearance tree trimmer** must be within **normal voice communication** when:

- The trimmer will approach **closer than 10 feet** to any conductor energized at **more than 750 volts**, or
- Branches or limbs being removed are **closer to lines energized at more than 750 V** than the MAD in the tables, or
- **Roping** is necessary to remove branches or limbs from such conductors or apparatus.

### Adverse Weather (1910.269(r)(1)(vi))

- **No** line-clearance tree trimming when adverse weather makes the work hazardous despite required work practices.
- **Examples:** Thunderstorms in the immediate vicinity, high winds, snow storms, ice storms.
- **Storm restoration:** Employees working in the aftermath of a storm (or similar emergency) must be **trained in the special hazards** of that work.
- If lines/circuits are **de-energized** per 1910.269(m), work may be performed in any weather.

## PPE and Insulating Equipment

- **Approved PPE** (including **fall protection** when needed) must be worn (1910.269(g)).
- **Climbing rope:** Minimum **12 mm (0.5 inch)** diameter; minimum breaking strength **10.2 kN (2,300 lb)**; synthetic rope elasticity **not more than 7%** (1910.269(r)(7)(i)).
- **Rope:** Do not use rope that is **wet**, **contaminated** (insulating capacity impaired), or **not insulated** for the voltage near exposed energized lines (1910.269(r)(7)(vii)).
- **Fall protection:** Each employee must be **tied in with climbing rope and safety saddle** when working above ground in a tree, **except** when ascending into the tree (1910.269(r)(8)).

## Equipment Safety (1910.269(r)(2)–(7))

### Brush chippers

- **Locking device** in the ignition system (r)(2)(i).
- **Access panels** for blades and drive train **in place and secure** during operation (r)(2)(ii).
- Trailer chippers detached from trucks **chocked or otherwise secured** (r)(2)(iv).
- PPE as required by **Subpart I** for employees at the feed table (r)(2)(v).

### Gasoline-engine power saws (r)(5)

- Saws **over 6.8 kg (15 lb)** service weight used in trees must be **supported by a separate line**, except from an aerial lift or during topping/removing when no supporting limb is available (r)(5)(i).
- **Clutch** adjusted so it will **not engage the chain drive at idling speed** (r)(5)(iii).
- **Control** that returns the saw to **idling speed** when released (r)(5)(ii).
- **Start on the ground or where firmly supported**; drop starting of saws over 6.8 kg (other than chain saws) only outside the bucket if area below is clear (r)(5)(iv).
- Engine **started and operated only when all other employees are clear** of the saw (r)(5)(v).
- Saw **may not be running** when being **carried up into a tree** (r)(5)(vi).
- **Stop engine** for cleaning, refueling, adjustments, and repairs unless manufacturer procedures say otherwise (r)(5)(vii).

### Rope (r)(7)

- **Inspect before each use**; do not use if unsafe (r)(7)(ii).
- **Do not splice** climbing rope to effect repair (r)(7)(vi).
- Store away from cutting edges and sharp tools; avoid contact with corrosive chemicals, gas, oil (r)(7)(iii).
- When stored, **coil and pile or suspend** so air can circulate (r)(7)(iv).
- **Secure rope ends** to prevent unraveling (r)(7)(v).

## Training and Proficiency

- Line-clearance tree trimmers must be **trained and certified** in, and **observed at least annually** complying with, **safety-related work practices and procedures**, including **emergency procedures** (1910.269).
- **Qualified employees** (269-qualified) must be trained per 1910.269(a)(2)(ii): e.g. what is safe to touch, maximum voltage, MAD for that voltage, use of protective equipment, recognition of electrical hazards and skills to control or avoid them.
- Until proficiency is demonstrated, employees in OJT must be **under the direct supervision** of a qualified employee.

---

## Emergency and Downed Lines

- **Downed or damaged lines:** Maintain safe distance; do not approach. Notify appropriate personnel (utility, supervisor, etc.) per company and utility procedures.
- **First aid** and **medical services** per 1910.269(b).
- **Unanticipated hazardous conditions** relating to electric lines and equipment must be reported (host/contract employer information transfer, 1910.269(a)(3)).
