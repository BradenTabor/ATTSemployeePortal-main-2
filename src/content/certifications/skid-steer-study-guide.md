# Skid Steer Certification — Study Guide

## Overview

This guide covers key topics for the Skid Steer certification test. Use it alongside hands-on training.

## Safety Distances

| Distance | Application |
|----------|-------------|
| 6 feet | From ledges, drop offs, and marked hazards |
| 10 feet | Buffer zone around poles and guy wires |
| 2 spans | Stay away from downed power lines |
| 300 feet | Personnel buffer zone during operation |
| 25 degrees | Maximum sideling ground operation |

## Safety Features

Know these Skid Steer safety features:

1. **Windshield** — Operator protection
2. **Seat belt** — Restraint system
3. **Horn** — Warning signal
4. **Backup beeper** — Alert when reversing
5. **Backup camera** — Safe reversing visibility

## Emergency Systems

### Hydraulic Release
- **Location**: Overhead on the cab
- **Purpose**: Release hydraulic pressure to bring head down during breakdown

### Emergency Exits
Two exits located:
1. **Front** of cab
2. **Rear back glass**

## Critical Safety Rules

- **No passengers** allowed in or on skid steer
- **Stop immediately** if anyone enters 300ft buffer zone
- **Seat belt required at all times** (not just rough terrain)
- Maintain **3 points of contact** ascending/descending
- **Wait for head to completely stop** before exiting cab

## Operation Limits

| Limit | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Max load height (grappling) | 3 feet |
| Max mulch head height | 3 feet |
| Max sideling | 25 degrees |

## Turning & Terrain

- Use **5-point turn** to protect maintained ground (yards)
- **Directly forward approach** for steep grades
- Stay 6 feet from ledges and drop offs

## Maintenance

### Grease Schedule
Every **8 hours**

### Track Tension
Proper play: **2½ inches**

### Sprocket Replacement
Replace when teeth wear in a **saw shape pattern**

## Responsibilities

### Hazard Identification
**Both** operator and groundman are responsible

### JSA
**Both** groundman and operator fill out daily JSA

## Downed Power Lines

If you find a downed power line:
1. Back away **two spans**
2. Notify operator
3. Notify anyone else in the area
4. Call General Foreman and O.C.

## Practical Evaluation Areas

### Machine Startup
- Equipment inspection
- JSA completion
- Proper PPE
- Fueling
- Warm-up time
- Function test

### Mulching/Grappling Observation
- Machine positioning
- Safety feature usage
- Safe mulching/grappling
- Communication (operator/groundman)
- 300ft distance maintained
- Line of sight maintained
- 10ft buffer from poles/guy wires

### Shutdown
- Park in suitable location
- Idle down with mowing head disengaged
- **Wait for head to stop** before exiting
- Machine properly shut down
- Debris/fire hazards removed
- Shutdown maintenance completed
